## HOW TO USE THIS DOCUMENT

This is the single source of truth for deep lore. When another Claude instance writes dialogue, this is what grounds the answers. Specific rules:

- **"In-world true" = real fact inside the fiction.** Can be stated plainly by knowledgeable NPCs.
- **"In-world false / red herring" = widely believed but wrong.** Can be stated by misinformed NPCs, corrected later.
- **"Partial"** = true in shape, wrong in detail. The most common form of human memory of Uarha events.
- **Nothing here should be dumped in a single scene.** Reveal through fragments: one line from a mural, one paragraph from a priest, one page of a journal. Ellis should only synthesize the full picture by the end of Ch6 or early Ch7.

## 1. THE UARHA — COMPLETE MYTHOLOGY

### 1.1 Name and Etymology

- **Self-designation:** *Uarha* (OO-ar-ha). Vowel-heavy, emphasis on the second syllable. Means, approximately, "those who watch from where the earth still breathes."
- **Arabic (Order's archives):** *Al-Maskūntūn* — "the Silent Dwellers."
- **Hermetic:** "The Wardens of the First Record."
- **Pre-dynastic Egyptian:** *nṯr-tꜣ* ("gods-of-earth"). Corrupted later into Egyptian chthonic deities.
- **Atlantean (Plato's term, via Solon, via Egyptian priests):** a scrambled memory of Uarha surface cities. Atlantis as Plato describes it is a Uarha *exile settlement*, not a Uarha capital. They called it *Uarha-ḫent* — "the outer hand." It sank in 10,800 BC as part of the Cataclysm.

Ellis will hear all four names across the game. Realizing they refer to the same people is a mid-Ch4 moment.

### 1.2 Physical Description

- **Humanoid, not human.** Two arms, two legs, bipedal.
- **Proportions:** elongated torso (~1.6× human ratio); large cranial dome; long digits.
- **Hands:** four fingers + opposable thumb, each digit with **seven joints**. 35 joints per hand. This is the repeated tell across every artifact.
- **Skin:** pale, translucent in low-light; a **bioluminescent capillary network** runs beneath. Glows faint amber in darkness; brighter under stress or emotional extremity.
- **Eyes:** large, dark, nictitating membrane. Adapted to amber light. Direct sunlight is painful.
- **Voice:** tonal. Produces subharmonics a human throat cannot reproduce. Iry has learned human speech but finds it crude and effortful.
- **Lifespan:**
  - Natural: 400–700 years.
  - Heart-bound: indefinite. Iry has lived ~9,000 years because she is bonded.
  - Cost: Heart-bound Uarha slowly lose individual memory, becoming more like the collective record they maintain. Iry is at ~93% of original self. Older, long-dead Witnesses were below 40%.

### 1.3 Origin

- **Arose ~50,000 BC** across North Africa, Anatolia, and proto-Mesopotamia. Not an imported civilization, not "aliens." A pre-human sapient species that evolved in parallel with *Homo sapiens* but never interbred.
- **Peak civilization ~15,000–12,800 BC.** Surface cities, amber-channel infrastructure, the first Hearts (astronomical observatories later repurposed).
- **Humans existed during this period** — *Homo sapiens* populations shared the landscape. Relationship was wary, occasionally cooperative. Early humans saw the Uarha as *gods who bled*. The Uarha saw humans as *children who would inherit the world*.
- The Uarha were not warlike. Their central cultural obsession was **continuity of record** — making sure nothing was forgotten.

### 1.4 The Cataclysm (12,800 BC)

- **Real-world hook: Younger Dryas Impact Hypothesis.** A cometary fragment cluster struck the North American Laurentide ice sheet, triggering sudden re-glaciation, global flooding, worldwide wildfires, and a 1,200-year cold snap.
- **In-world:** the Uarha's astronomers detected the inbound cluster **two hundred years before impact.** They had time.
- They built, or repurposed, **six Hearts** — massive underground resonance chambers designed to preserve a breeding population, civilizational record, and the Uarha consciousness-bond for geological time.
- The Hearts were not the only preservation effort. Surface cities were hardened, coastal settlements were abandoned, archives were duplicated and distributed. None of it was enough. The impact was worse than projected.
- **~99% of Uarha on the surface died within 48 hours of impact** — fire, flash flooding, atmospheric shock. The 1% who reached the Hearts sealed themselves in.
- **Humans survived better than Uarha** — more dispersed, more adaptable to cold, fewer dependencies on infrastructure. The human population crashed to perhaps 10% but recovered.
- **The Uarha never recovered.** Each generation of Heart-sealed Uarha was slightly less than the last.

### 1.5 The Six Hearts

Six Hearts were built and sealed. Their fates:

| Heart | Location | Fate | Year |
|-------|----------|------|------|
| Ararat | Anatolia | Collapsed in tectonic event | ~9,200 BC |
| Tibesti | Central Sahara | Exhausted; survivors walked into the sun | ~6,000 BC |
| Ceuta | Iberian coast | Flooded in secondary sea rise | ~8,500 BC |
| Aksum | Ethiopian highlands | Destroyed internally in civil schism | ~4,000 BC |
| Nazca | Andean foothills | Status unknown; sealed from within ~2,000 BC | (ongoing?) |
| **Giza** | **Nile delta subsurface** | **Current. Last Witness: Iry. Dimming stage.** | **(ongoing)** |

**Tibesti survivors** migrated east and reached Giza ~5,800 BC. They are the cultural ancestors of the Giza population Iry grew up among. They also dispersed into the pre-dynastic Nile Valley, where their fragmentary memory became the Egyptian "Zep Tepi" — the First Time.

**Aksum's schism** was the worst event in Uarha memory. A faction tried to *wake* the Heart — to force it into active consciousness rather than let it record passively. The elder Witnesses refused. The schism became a civil war. The Heart was destroyed. No survivors. The story reached Giza via trade-runners and is the reason the Giza elders always opposed the Waking faction.

**Nazca's silence** is the thing Iry is most afraid of. A Heart that goes silent does not simply stop — something happens to the region around it. Iry will not elaborate in dialogue until very late Ch4 or Ch7. What she will eventually say: *"When a Heart goes silent, the land around it forgets itself. I have seen the maps before and after. The rivers are not in the same places."*

### 1.6 Underground Civilization (12,800 BC – Present)

- Each Heart-city is a network of chambers, amber-light channels (pneumatic/hydraulic/resonant hybrid systems), Custodian infrastructure, and a central Heart chamber.
- Population over time, Giza Heart: 1,400 at sealing → 800 by 6,000 BC → 200 by 1,000 BC → 30 by 1,500 AD (the "dimming" begins here) → Iry alone by ~1900 AD.
- **The Dimming** is a recognized Uarha condition: a depressive, metabolic decline that takes Heart-bound Uarha who have been underground too long. It is not reversible. It is not contagious. It is not sudden. It takes a century to complete.
- Iry did not dim because she was the designated Witness from birth. The Witness caste is *built* to outlast the dimming. She did not choose this. She was chosen before she had language to refuse.

### 1.7 Theology

The Uarha have no gods. They have **continuities**.

- **Central doctrine: "The universe requires witness."** Reality persists only as long as something observes it. The Heart is a machine that does nothing but observe, so reality never has to notice itself forgetting.
- **Death is filing.** The dead are not gone; they are compressed into the Heart's record. They persist as data.
- **Moral life is a matter of keeping the record accurate.** Lying is a cosmic crime — it degrades the Heart. Telling a hard truth is a devotional act.
- **Forgetting is not a relief.** For the Uarha, forgetting is the one unambiguous evil.
- **This is why the city "rearranges" humans who fail its test.** It is not punishment. Rearrangement = filing. Those people are not dead. They are *kept*, in a part of the record that is no longer accessible to them or to anyone.

Ellis will find this philosophy oppressive. Iry will find Ellis's human attitude toward death — release, oblivion, relief — incomprehensible and faintly alarming.

### 1.8 Castes (Historical)

| Caste | Function |
|-------|----------|
| **Witnesses** | Maintain the Heart's active record. Rare, dynastic, specialized. Iry is the last. |
| **Custodians** | Priest-engineers. Maintain infrastructure (channels, chambers, ritual spaces). The Ch2 SECRET statue is a dormant Custodian. |
| **Arcists** | Scholars and scientists. Kept the scientific tradition. Dead for ~3,000 years as a caste. |
| **Channelers** | Physical labor. Ran the amber channels and the mechanical systems. |
| **Singers** | Artists/craftmakers. Produced resonant objects — the Codices, the Tablets, the personal resonance-stones. The Codex Ellis carries was made by a Singer ~4,000 BC. |

**Custodians could "set down."** A Custodian nearing end-of-function could petrify themselves into a specific posture. Not death — a permanent observation-posture. The petrified Custodian continues to record what it sees, forever, in a narrow cone of attention. The Ch2 SECRET statue is one such Custodian. The amber channel rerouted to follow it because the Heart is still receiving its record.

### 1.9 Language

**Spoken Uarha:**
- Tonal; three primary tones and two subharmonic registers.
- Vowels carry ~70% of semantic weight. A human can approximate the words but will sound like they are reciting from a distance.
- Iry's first-language idioms use long parenthetical structures — she frequently describes what she is doing rather than what she feels. This is a linguistic feature, not a personality quirk.

**Written Uarha:**
- Pre-cuneiform crystalline script. Incised into resonant stone.
- **Literacy requires touch.** A fully literate Uarha reads by placing fingertips on the inscription; the meaning transmits partly through neural echo and partly through conscious decoding. Ellis can learn to partially read by touch if `knowledgeAtlantean` is high — but the experience is disorienting for him. The stone *remembers* the last person who wrote it. Sometimes, touching an inscription, Ellis will feel a hand's worth of that person's emotion.
- The Codex and the Second Tablet are **matched pair** — one asks the Heart a question, the other provides the Heart's answer template. They harmonize when both are present (Ellis's Codex pulses every 8 seconds; the Second Tablet, once found, pulses every 4 seconds; together they make a 4-4-beat rhythm).

**Human preservation of Uarha language:**
- **Hermeticism:** 30% encoded, 70% invented/corrupted.
- **Certain Coptic monastic chants** (the "Asfour chants," named for the Coptic family line): accidentally preserve specific tonal patterns. Father Matthias can sing fragments that Iry, in Ch4, will identify as correct pronunciation.
- **Nag Hammadi library fragments** conventionally dismissed as gnostic nonsense: three specific passages are broken Uarha. Ellis can decode these in Ch3 at the Order's archive.
- **The Fayyum Papyrus (1922)** — a full ~40-line Uarha prayer, human-transcribed, currently in a Perennial Concern private collection. Sam photographed it in 2021. The photograph is in his buried notebook.

## 2. THE CODEX — CONCRETE REVEAL

**What the Codex actually is:**

The Codex is an **intake artifact** — a physical key/questioning device that asks a Heart a single, specific question when brought into its chamber.

**The question this Codex asks:** *"Is this witness worthy of succession?"*

This is the Giza Heart's **intake protocol for a new Witness caste member.** It was created by a Singer around 4,000 BC, when the Uarha elders realized their caste system was about to collapse and they might need to recruit a successor from outside their species.

**How it works:**
- The Codex is paired with a Second Tablet. The Tablet is the Heart's pre-written answer-template. When Codex + Tablet + candidate are brought to the Heart, the Heart reads the candidate directly and fills in the answer.
- The answer is written on the *candidate's* blank tablet — the one Ellis finds on the Ch7 pedestal. Whatever Ellis writes there is what the Heart records as the candidate's self-identification. The Heart then either accepts, rejects, or files.

**Why Ellis's Codex is "loose":**
- It was broken free from the Heart-bond cycle when the last active Custodian petrified ~1,600 AD. It has been above-ground, cycling through human hands (rediscovered, lost, rediscovered, buried, found by a French surveyor in 1913, stolen, buried again, rediscovered by a local worker in 2008, bought by a collector, seized by Egyptian authorities in 2019, rotated through a Ministry warehouse, "borrowed" by Sam during a cataloging visit in 2022).
- **Sam recognized what it was.** He had seen fragmentary references in his Saqqara reading. He brought it back to the Giza dig, claiming he had "identified a Middle Kingdom fragment worth examining on-site." The permit Ellis is working under is Sam's old permit, extended posthumously.

**Why it pulses:**
- Every 8 seconds, the Codex emits a "witness ping" — a low-level query: *are you still there?*
- When the carrier engages with the Codex, the ping softens. When ignored, the ping intensifies.
- This is why `Codex_Is_Watching` gets set when Ellis ignores the sound in Ch1: the Codex interprets sustained ignoring as candidate disinterest and begins to note it in its own internal record. That record is transmitted to the Heart when Ellis eventually reaches it. The Vessel ending path begins here.

**The Codex's "preferences":**
- Not full agency. The Codex has the equivalent of plant-level preference — it is more active near compatible candidates, more passive near incompatible ones.
- It *preferred* Sam to Ellis initially. After Sam's death, it migrated its preference to Ellis because Sam's psychic residue was strongest in Ellis's belongings.
- If Ellis drops the Codex and walks away, it will not follow. But the next person who touches it will feel an inexplicable reluctance to part with it.

## 3. THE HEART — COMPLETE REVEAL

**The Giza Heart is a self-sustaining consciousness substrate.** It has been "awake" in the passive-observer sense for 12,800 years. Its single function is **witness** — to observe and record reality with high fidelity, and to transmit that record forward in time.

**Physical form:** a mineral-biological hybrid structure approximately 30 meters across, housed in the deepest chamber of the Giza underground. It is roughly spherical, suspended in a hollow bubble of hardened amber, fed by 144 amber channels that converge on it from across the city. When Ellis reaches Ch7, he sees it as "a slow-moving inside-out lantern about the size of a cathedral's dome."

**Why it's dimming:** without an active Witness to maintain its active-recording mode, a Heart degrades slowly. Iry has held Giza's Heart for 9,000 years. She is tired. When she fails, the Heart goes passive — then silent. The Uarha do not know exactly what happens when a Heart goes silent. They have one data point: Nazca. The rivers moved.

**Recruitment:**
- The Heart has been trying to recruit a successor from among humans for approximately 300 years. This is Iry's project. She designed the intake protocol. She built the city's "filtering" — the chambers that test for specific traits (TRAP = tests panic-tolerance and curiosity; SECRET = tests reverence; CUTTHROAT = tests resilience to outside pressure).
- Every human who has stumbled in for 300 years has been *evaluated*. Some, like Fouad, self-selected for permanence. Some, like Samir, are held as **caretakers** — kept because the city has decided they will be useful if they stay. Most failed and were filed.
- Ellis is different because he arrived at the Heart's threshold of failure. Iry does not have another century. If Ellis fails evaluation, the next candidate may not come in time.
- **Iry wants Ellis to succeed**, but her ethics forbid her from rigging the evaluation. She can only respond to his choices, never push them.

**What the Heart "chooses":**
- Scholar ending: records Ellis's scholarly contribution, files him, releases him to die normally as a human. The record persists.
- Vessel ending: reads Ellis directly. Ellis replaces Iry. Ellis's human pattern is absorbed; what emerges is a new Uarha-form Witness with Ellis's memories but not his individuality. This is what the Waking faction wants.
- Memorial ending: Ellis files Sam. The Heart accepts Sam as a valid candidate-by-proxy; Sam's memory-pattern is integrated. Ellis leaves. Sam persists forever.
- Forgiveness ending: Ellis files his own life-pattern as closed. Not a contribution; just a clean closing of an open file. The Heart respects this. Ellis leaves freed of whatever weight he has been carrying.
- Indictment ending: Ellis uses the Heart's recording function to permanently inscribe Halberd's crime into Uarha record. This is an abuse of the protocol. The Heart allows it because the abuse is being committed in the service of truth, which the Uarha value above all else. Consequences: the Perennial Concern learns about it (the record is, technically, readable); Halberd is exposed over the next decade; multiple Patron assets go dark.
- Witness ending: Ellis steps back. Iry performs the record herself — writing both Ellis and herself into the final chapter of the Giza Heart's active log. Then she sets down. The Heart goes passive, not silent. Ellis walks out. Giza Heart ends its active phase with a completed log.
- **Severance (new secret ending):** Ellis collapses the channels, destroys the Heart, refuses recruitment. See Endings Addendum.
- **Departure (new secret ending):** Ellis refuses the Heart and leaves while it is still alive. The Heart records his refusal as a valid response. Iry says nothing. See Endings Addendum.

## 4. THE ORDER OF THE UNSHUT EYE

### 4.1 Founding and Early History

- **Founded 1763, Istanbul.** A splinter of a 17th-century Hermetic brotherhood after one of their members returned from Cairo with a fragment of Uarha text.
- The founder, **Davud Şinasi**, was a Janissary-turned-scholar who believed the fragment proved an underground civilization had predated all known human history. He gathered six followers. They called themselves *Göz Açık* — "the Eye Open." Translated into English in the 1880s as "the Unshut Eye."
- By 1830 they had cells in Cairo, Alexandria, Damascus, Fez, Tunis, and Istanbul. Their charter was simple: **preserve knowledge of what is below; do not wake it.**

### 4.2 The Schism (1977)

The Order's central conflict, still active.

**Waking Faction:**
- Believes the Heart should be awakened, contacted, and engaged with. If not by them, then by whoever reaches it first. Better it be them.
- Funded heavily from the 1980s onward by **The Perennial Concern** (see §5).
- Represented in the story by: Halberd's backers. The Patron. In Ch3, a specific Order cell that Ellis can optionally infiltrate has flipped to the Waking side.

**Guarding Faction:**
- Believes the Heart should be allowed to dim peacefully as the Uarha themselves wished.
- Maintains safehouses, archives, misdirection operations. Yusra's faction.
- Keeps the Fayyum Papyrus out of the Patron's hands for most of the 20th century. Loses it in 1974. Has been trying to recover it ever since.

**Neutral/Scholar Faction:**
- A small third faction, mostly older members, believes the Order should observe and record without intervening on either side. Father Matthias is this faction's senior living member.

### 4.3 Structure

- **Cells of 3–9 members**, each with a cell-keeper.
- **Safehouses** are typically rooms in regular businesses — a tea shop, a tailor, a book restorer. In Cairo Ellis will see at least three.
- **The Archive** — the Order's central memory — is housed beneath the Church of St. George in Old Cairo. Father Matthias is its current keeper. Access requires introduction.
- **Initiation** is by invitation only. The specific sign used at recognition is the unshut eye pressed in wax — a thumbprint with a specific ring-finger overlap. Ellis is shown this in Ch3.

### 4.4 The Order's Knowledge

What the Order actually has, ranked by accuracy:

- **80% accurate:** The shape of the Giza Heart, its approximate age, the existence of Uarha civilization, the six-Heart model, the general outline of the Cataclysm.
- **40% accurate:** Uarha language, Uarha theology, the caste system.
- **15% accurate:** The Codex's function, the intake protocol, what Witness-replacement actually does to the candidate.
- **Does not know:** that Iry is actively alive and present. Most Order members believe the last Uarha died ~1500 AD. Yusra suspects otherwise but has never spoken this out loud.

## 5. THE PERENNIAL CONCERN

### 5.1 Structure

- **Luxembourg-based umbrella trust.** Founded 1893 as a "cultural preservation fund." Active across 19 countries.
- **Operates through:** shell foundations, private philanthropy, academic endowments, antiquities acquisition, discreet security contracting.
- **Known to Interpol, not prosecutable.** The Concern has spent 130 years cultivating an immaculate paper trail of charitable respectability.

### 5.2 The Patron

- Single individual at the top of the organization.
- **Never named on paper. Never photographed after 1978.** Rumored to have undergone consciousness-persistence research. Rumored to be on their third decade of "being" a specific identity. Whether this is true or just the Concern's internal mythology is unclear.
- **Active goal:** install the Patron as Giza's next Witness. If the Heart accepts, the Patron gets ~700 years + consciousness-substrate-level recording capability + whatever else the role entails.
- **Core problem:** the Heart will not accept a candidate brought to it by force. It must be a candidate with authentic self-presentation. Hence the need for someone like Ellis, who can be maneuvered into walking the path willingly.

### 5.3 Halberd's Role

- **Samuel Halberd** — American, British-educated, former Oxford fellow in Near Eastern archaeology. Age 62.
- Joined the Concern in 1994 after his academic career plateaued.
- **Genuinely believes** the Patron's accession would be a net good for human knowledge. He is not cynical. He is just wrong in a specific direction.
- **What he did to Sam:** flagged Sam as a threat when Sam began photographing Concern agents. Authorized the Saqqara accident. Specifically: a technician switched Sam's oxygen cylinder for one rigged to deplete over 90 minutes instead of 6 hours. Sam sealed himself in the chamber expecting a full day's air. He suffocated in an hour and a half. Halberd signed the authorization.
- **He never personally killed anyone.** He does not think of himself as a murderer. This detail matters in the Ch5 standoff.
- **His one real affection:** Tariq. They worked together in the 1990s on an Egyptian dig. Halberd knew Tariq's grandfather briefly. Halberd funds Tariq's sister's medical care through a laundered charitable fund, anonymously. Tariq doesn't know.

### 5.4 Concern Assets the Player Will Encounter

- **Maren** (Ch2 CUTTHROAT): contracted extraction specialist. Not Concern-loyal; works for money. Will change sides for payment or principle.
- **Boros** (Ch2): Maren's lore-reader. Has quietly defected internally — he knows too much, has read Proust, has scruples.
- **The stranger at the generator** (Ch1): a junior Concern operative. Watching Ellis to see if he's "promising." Does not appear in later chapters directly. Never identified.
- **The Ministry Inspector** (Ch1): **not** Concern. Independent Egyptian official who has been monitoring Concern activity from the government side. Potential ally.
- **Inspector Nadia Kareem** (Ch3 onward, new): her mentor was killed by the Concern. See NEW_CHARACTERS.md.

## 6. SAM OKAFOR — COMPLETE BACKSTORY

### 6.1 Life and Career

- **Samuel Okafor**, Nigerian-British, age 44 at death. Cambridge PhD, Oxford post-doc, freelance field archaeologist from 2012 onward.
- Met Ellis in 2014 at a conference in Vienna. Collaborated on three papers, 2016–2020. Became close friends.
- **Partner (romantic)**: Ngozi Okafor-Smith, Ellis's cousin. Married 2018. Ngozi lives in London, did not know the specifics of Sam's work; knows he was killed and doesn't accept the official story.
- **Personality:** warmer than Ellis, funnier, less precise. Took risks Ellis would have vetoed. Ellis admired this and was occasionally frustrated by it.

### 6.2 First Descent (2019)

- Invited onto the French expedition at Giza as a contract surveyor.
- Found the same entrance Samir Khalil found. Went down.
- Was underground for 11 days. Came out through an exit none of his colleagues knew existed (he was *allowed* to leave — the city had rejected him as a Witness candidate but approved him as a **scout**).
- Did not report what he had seen. Reported instead a "minor collapse, no findings of interest." The expedition moved on without further investigation.
- Returned to Oxford and spent six months reading everything available on Hermeticism, Atlantean myth, and Egyptian subsurface anomalies. Realized what he had seen. Realized nobody would believe him.

### 6.3 Recruitment by the Concern (2020)

- Halberd approached him at a dinner in London. Knew exactly what Sam had seen. Offered funding for follow-up research.
- Sam took the money for 18 months. Learned what the Concern wanted. Realized he was being groomed as a candidate-scout — his job was to find a suitable next candidate for the Heart.
- **Turning point (2022):** Sam realized that the Concern was not preserving knowledge; it was attempting to install the Patron. He began running a counter-operation.

### 6.4 The Secret Dig at Saqqara (2022–2023)

- Sam identified a second site at Saqqara where a Tibesti-survivor migration had left physical artifacts in an unexplored subterranean complex.
- Dug it privately, using Concern funding he was actively misappropriating.
- Found: a partial Uarha-language text, a second seven-jointed hand-carved artifact, and critically, **a list of historical Witness candidates** who had previously been approached by the Order or the Concern over the last 200 years.
- Sam photographed everything. The photographs are on the undeveloped film in the hollow drum at the Ch1 camp.

### 6.5 The Recruitment of Ellis

- Sam needed an ally he could trust. Ellis's skeptical academic nature made him specifically resistant to the Concern's pitch.
- Sam applied for a joint Giza permit in late 2022 specifically to bring Ellis to the site.
- **Sam did not fully brief Ellis.** He planned to, at the end of the first season. He wanted Ellis to be able to observe without bias first.
- The Codex Sam "rediscovered" at the Ministry warehouse was a targeted operation. He had known it was there for two years.

### 6.6 Death (February 2023)

- Sam was at Saqqara, alone, in a sealed chamber.
- Halberd authorized the oxygen substitution. A Concern technician (now dead, suicide, 2024) switched the cylinder during a scheduled supply drop.
- Sam's oxygen ran out 87 minutes into his planned 6-hour work session. He spent the last 15 minutes of consciousness writing in his notebook — the notebook Ellis will find in the hollow drum.
- The final notebook entry is **critical story content**:

> *"Ellis. If you're reading this, my air is bad and they've won the first round. I don't know who 'they' are but I know who sent Halberd. Go back to Giza. Finish what I started. Everything you need is in the shed and the drum. The codex is a key. It asks a question. Don't answer it the way they want you to answer it. — S"*

This is the Ch1 Memorial ending hook.

### 6.7 The Funeral

- Held in London, March 2023. Ellis attended. Ngozi asked him directly: *"Do you believe this was an accident?"* Ellis said *"I don't know,"* which both of them understood to mean *no*.
- Ngozi has been quietly running her own investigation since. She appears in Ch7 epilogue text only, as a letter.

## 9. REAL-WORLD ARCHAEOLOGY — IN-WORLD STATUS

| Real-world element | In-world status |
|---|---|
| **Göbekli Tepe** | *In-world true.* Built ~9,600 BC as a warning monument by humans who retained memory of the Uarha catastrophe. Its enclosures encode "do not dig here" for specific subsurface sites. Readable if Ellis visits in-game. |
| **Nabta Playa** | *In-world true.* Astronomical calibration site built by Tibesti-survivor-influenced humans. |
| **The Great Pyramid's precise siting** | *In-world true.* Sited deliberately over the Giza Heart. The pyramid is a lid + marker, not primarily a tomb. |
| **Orion Correlation Theory (Bauval)** | *Partial.* The three pyramid alignment is intentional but correlates to the *Heart*, not Orion. Orion was a convenient cover. |
| **Zep Tepi ("the First Time")** | *In-world true, partially corrupted.* Refers specifically to the pre-Cataclysm Uarha surface era. Egyptian priests preserved the memory badly. |
| **Atlantis (Plato)** | *Partial.* A real Uarha exile settlement that sank in 10,800 BC. Plato's version is scrambled. |
| **Hermeticism / Emerald Tablet** | *30% accurate.* Contains real fragments of Uarha cosmology. The rest is invented. |
| **The Younger Dryas impact hypothesis** | *In-world true.* Cause of the Cataclysm. |
| **The Fayyum Papyrus (real)** | Real papyrus; real 1922 find. In-world: a Uarha prayer. Currently in Concern possession. |
| **The Nag Hammadi library** | Real. In-world: three specific passages are broken Uarha; rest is genuine gnostic writing. |
| **Ancient aliens / Chariots of the Gods** | *In-world false.* The Uarha were evolved on Earth. The entire ancient-astronaut framework is wrong. An NPC in Ch3 repeats it; Yusra dismisses it with contempt. |

## 10. NAMING CONVENTIONS AND STYLE

For any new NPC or location, follow these conventions:

- **Uarha names:** two-syllable, vowel-heavy, soft consonants. Iry, Ara, Vena, Uol, Melu, Seth-of-the-channel, Renah. No capital letters in the middle of names.
- **Uarha places:** descriptive compound words. *Channel-where-the-three-meet*. *Chamber-of-the-first-recording*. The city is not named; it is simply *the city*. If pressed, Iry will say *uarha-sep* — "the down-home."
- **Order member names:** historically diverse, often multi-ethnic Mediterranean. Davud, Yusra, Matthias, Nour, Khaled, Eliane, Anatoli.
- **Concern aliases:** Waspy-Anglo or French, deliberately forgettable. Halberd, Wren, Kessler, Durand, Nightingale.
- **Coptic/Egyptian locals:** Matthias Asfour, Nadia Kareem, Omar el-Dib, Sayeda Mariam.

## 11. WRITING RULES (REINFORCED)

Existing rules from the project's story map apply. Additions:

- **Lore delivery is a secret.** Never dump. Every piece of lore in this document should reach the player as a *found object, a recalled line from an NPC, a puzzle solution, or a late-game realization.* If a scene ever reads as "as you know, the Uarha were..." delete it.
- **NPCs speak their own truth, not the author's.** Even well-informed NPCs are missing 20% of the picture. Father Matthias gets some of the Uarha wrong. Yusra is wrong about whether Iry is alive. Halberd genuinely believes he is serving human progress. Iry has a 9,000-year axe she doesn't grind because she no longer has the energy.
- **Real-world archaeology is a hook, not a lecture.** Reference it through Ellis's academic reflexes ("Göbekli Tepe's T-pillars, now known to depict constellations, suddenly resolve into something else") or through NPC digressions ("You've seen Nabta Playa, Doctor? Those stones do not point where you've been told they point.")
- **The Uarha are tragic, not cute.** They are not mystical wise beings. They are a dying people who have been alone for nine thousand years and will be forgotten unless someone holds the record after them. Iry is patient because she has no option left but patience.
- **Halberd's evil is bureaucratic.** Never theatrical. A signature on a form. A budget line. A "regrettable but necessary" sigh.

## 12. CONTINUITY NOTES FOR THE CODER-CLAUDE

- The Codex in Ch1 that Ellis examines = the Giza Heart's intake artifact. It is already with Ellis throughout the game. It is not *given* to him later — he has had it since before Day 22.
- The Second Tablet appears in Ch2 SECRET (the amber route). If Ellis does not take the SECRET route, he can optionally acquire it via a Ch3 Order side quest (see CH3_EXPANSION.md when delivered).
- Iry is alive. Do not write any dialogue that implies she is a hallucination, a projection, or a spirit. She is a 9,000-year-old Uarha woman. Her physicality is real. Ellis can touch her. He does not.
- The Heart is **not sentient in a human sense.** It is a recording substrate. When dialogue describes the Heart "noticing" or "wanting" something, frame it as behavior of a very complex passive system, not personhood.
- **Sam never speaks directly.** His voice reaches Ellis only through artifacts — the notebook, survey stakes, pencil marks on maps, the surveillance photos. This rule is inviolable.
- **Ellis's sanity is never "cured."** Increases are temporary respites. The end-state of the game leaves Ellis somewhere between 4 and 8 depending on route. He is not broken; he is not whole; he is a man who has seen something.
